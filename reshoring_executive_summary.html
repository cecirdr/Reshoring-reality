The environmental cost nobody discusses
Full domestic
mining would mean
radioactive waste

Rare earth processing generates thorium and uranium co-extraction waste. Lithium mining destroys arid aquifers that supply American agriculture. The world distributes this industrial burden across many nations — each absorbing a manageable share — for a very good reason. Concentrating it domestically is not patriotism. It's sacrifice.
Source: USGS · EPA · Mountain Pass closure history · Columbia CGEP

China's hidden vulnerability
1.01
China's fertility rate —
among the lowest ever
recorded for a large economy
Replacement level is 2.1. China's working-age population has been declining since 2011. The researchers dominating industrial R&D today were born under the one-child policy. The pipeline is already smaller, and every cohort behind it is smaller still. China has a 15–20 year window before demographic contraction begins to erode what it has built.
Same window the US has to build its allied alternative · Same clock · Opposite problem
Source: UN World Population Prospects 2024 · RAND Corp 2025 · NBS China
The one chokepoint that works
0
EUV lithography machines
China is permitted to have
One Dutch company — ASML — builds every EUV machine on Earth. Without it, you cannot manufacture a leading-edge chip. China has 5–8× more semiconductor researchers than the US and still cannot make an advanced AI chip because of this single export control. This is the only western technology restriction that is genuinely decisive. The Netherlands is a non-negotiable ally.
ASML's EUV machines cost $350M each · Zero substitutes exist anywhere in the world
Source: ASML · CSIS · SIA · Atlantic Council 2025

The only rational path forward
Strategic
interdependence
— not autarky
Globalization distributed industrial burden across many nations — each absorbing a manageable share, each gaining economic development. Dismantling that network faster than replacements can be built creates a window of maximum vulnerability. The answer is a coordinated allied supply web — Australia, Canada, Chile, Japan, South Korea, Taiwan, the EU — that compresses a 30–40 year solo timeline to 10–20 years. The obstacles are not technological. They are political. Specifically: the willingness to treat allies as allies.
